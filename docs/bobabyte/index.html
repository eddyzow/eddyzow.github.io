Ready for a bobatastic adventure of creativity?

BobaByte is an up-and-coming all-day social coding event for middle and high schoolers, to be held at TBD on October 26, 2024. Scratch, Java, HTML - everyone from newbies to experienced coders are invited! It's absolutely free to attend. Projects will be judged on many categories, so everyone has a chance to win awesome prizes. And let's not forget the best part - free food, merch, and boba will be served!

A classic hackathon experience with a twist.

We're hoping to see people who have never attended a social coding event (i.e. hackathon) before come to BobaByte. We'll be offering workshops on Scratch apps, Java apps, CAD design, and HTML. There's plenty to learn for everyone!

A reputable team.

BobaByte is led by a team of high school seniors with national-level awards in STEM and robotics. We're taking care of all the logistics - you just have to show up and have fun!

Everything's free.

Food, merch, and boba served during the day are all fully covered by our amazing sponsors, who have made BobaByte possible. You can even get reimbursement for travel to BobaByte!

Amazing prizes.

We'll be judging based on categories from Coolest Project and Most Useful to Judges' Choice and more. There's a category for everyone! The winners will receive anything from gift cards and goodies to super cool robotics kits!

About the Team Behind BobaByte

We're Aprameya, Eddy, Rick, and Tanish, four seniors at Acton-Boxborough Regional High School (Acton, MA) with a mission to bring a high-quality coding event to the Greater Boston area. We believe that with today's world becoming increasingly digital, it is crucial for youth to become well-versed in programming for success in the modern workforce. We wanted to set up an event that would give middle and high schoolers the opportunity to learn and win prizes, plus enjoy the best drink ever - bubble tea!

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Who can participate?

Anyone in grades 7-12! We will be running two age divisions of awards: Division I (grades 7-9) and Division II (grades 10-12).

How much does it cost?

Free! Our generous sponsors will fund all merchandise, food, boba, and prizes. Hack Club's Gas Fund may even cover travel costs.

Can I work with my friends?

Teams of up to 4 people are accepted! You can make new friends when you arrive, bring your own, or work alone!

What can I make?

Code any project you can make within the eight hours of BobaByte! A video game, a webapp, a Scratch project - the possibilities are endless.

What kinds of prizes will there be?

Each winner will receive a gift card, a box of electronics parts, and more! Even if you don't win an award, you'll still take home a goodie bag full of BobaByte swag! Final prizes are to be determined.

What types of projects will win awards?

We will be giving out six awards per division: Best Beginner, Fan Favorite, Judges' Choice, Most Creative, Most Technical, and Most Useful.

What's a hackathon? Is BobaByte a hackathon?

A traditional hackathon is an event where teams "hack" (code projects) for a period of time, often 24 hours. These projects are then judged, and the winners receive prizes. BobaByte is like a hackathon, but the projects are smaller (8 hours) and we will be hosting workshops throughout the day to help young people of all skill levels.

What if I don't have any coding experience?

That's totally fine! This social coding event is about teaching people how awesome coding is. Our Programming Leads, experienced in HTML, JavaScript, CSS, C++, Java, Python, and Scratch, can help guide you in the right direction! If your project strikes a chord in the judges' hearts, you might even win Best Beginner!

Have more questions? Feel free to email us at team@bobabyte.org. Note: this email address is not active yet.
